Reducing Cognitive Load in Clinical Decision-Making
with GenAI-Powered Report Analysis
Gideon Kowadlo  ·  Yoel Mittelberg  ·  Daniel Stiglitz
Atidia
Background
Clinicians managing complex patients must manually navigate multiple longitudinal test reports — a cognitively demanding process that makes it difficult to identify trends and synthesise findings at the point of care. Over time, patients accumulate results across many modalities, and the burden of integrating these findings can lead to missed insights and suboptimal clinical decisions.
Aims
To develop a GenAI-based system that automatically extracts, summarises, and visualises trends from patient test reports, and surfaces personalised best-practice clinical recommendations — evaluated here on respiratory function tests and echocardiograms.
Methods
A model-agnostic LLM iteratively processes patients' test reports to extract key clinical metrics, plot trends, and generate summaries. Prompts include guardrails to avoid hallucinations. Best-practice recommendations are codified and dynamically presented via a rule-based look-up mechanism.

Results
The system analysed reports, extracted key information, and presented trends in plot and table formats. Text-based summaries and best-practice clinical recommendations were generated and reviewed by an experienced anaesthetist, who found results to be accurate.
Conclusions
This proof-of-concept demonstrates that a GenAI-based system can accurately synthesise longitudinal patient reports and surface personalised best-practice recommendations, significantly reducing clinician cognitive load. Results support proceeding to a prospective clinical trial.
Future Work
  • RAG-based dynamic best-practice guideline lookup
  • Clinician chat interface for interactive report discussion
  • Prospective clinical trial evaluation
